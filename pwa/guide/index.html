RANGEFINDER

Striving to be the best part of your day.

A GPS rangefinder built for one course — every hole, hazard, and green at White Bear Yacht Club, in your pocket and working offline. Here's the whole app in two screens.

1 · The main screen

Stand over your ball; the phone becomes the ball. Everything you need for the shot lives here.

  1. Tee selector. Tap your tee — the yardage line and the GPS‑OFF start point follow. Red & Green score against women's par.
  2. CENTER distance. The big number is yards to the middle of the green. Under it, "plays like" folds in elevation, wind, and your club (e.g. plays 152 · 7i). Tap the box for a side‑profile of the shot.
  3. Map views. SAT aerial · GRN green zoom · PIN drop today's pin · 3D flyover with an animated tracer.
  4. Wind arrow. Points where the wind blows relative to the hole; the number is mph. Up = tailwind to the green.
  5. You. The glowing gold ball is your GPS position — walk and it walks with you. A pink "finding you…" marker shows until it locks on.
  6. Shot tracking. Set the club (7i = 7‑iron), tap SHOT at your ball. The app learns your real distances; long‑press to undo.
  7. Score strip. TOTAL SCORE and PUTTS for this hole, big +/− buttons. Every tap saves itself.
  8. CARD ▸. Your running total and holes played — tap to open the full scorecard (screen 2).
  9. Status card. Green dot + "GPS ±5m" = tracking you. WIND / ± ELEV toggle the "plays like" math; GPS ON/OFF switches live tracking vs. tap‑to‑place.
  10. Hazards & targets. Carry and reach distances to every bunker, water hazard, and dogleg on the hole.

2 · The scorecard

Tap CARD ▸ to open your round — styled like the printed club card, with three views along the top.

  1. Three views. CARD is your hole‑by‑hole round; CLUBS shows your learned distance per club; STATS is your round history, trend line, and bests.
  2. Tap a hole to jump. Any hole row takes the app straight to that hole. Your current hole is highlighted.
  3. OUT / IN / TOTAL. Front nine, back nine, and total — strokes only, no depressing over‑par tally. Red/Green rounds score women's par automatically.
  4. POST. Copies your hole‑by‑hole scores and opens ghin.com to paste — a deliberate two‑tap handoff, so your GHIN login never touches the app.
  5. NEW. Banks the finished round into your history (bests, averages, club distances all grow from this), then clears the card.
  6. Export / Import. Back up your whole record to a file, or move it to a new phone. Rounds otherwise live only on the device that recorded them — no accounts, by design.

3 · Getting started

Install it on your phone (~40 seconds)

  1. Open wbyc-rangefinder.netlify.app in Safari (iPhone) or Chrome (Android).
  2. Tap Allow when it asks for your location.
  3. Tap the Share button → Add to Home Screen → Add.
  4. Launch it from the home‑screen icon after that — full screen, offline, screen stays awake during a round.

Your name & your record

Tap + your name once (top right) — it signs your card and stays on the device. Then just play: every score and shot saves itself. At the last green, open the card and tap NEW to bank the round — that's the move that builds your trend, bests, and club distances.

Is the GPS working? Outdoors, the status card goes green and reads GPS ±5m, and the gold ball sits where you stand. Before it locks on you'll see a pink finding you… marker — normal. Always open the app from the web link (not an old saved copy), tap Allow for location, and be under open sky.

5 · Good to know

Pinch to zoom

Two fingers zoom the hole map up to 8× and pan; pinch back out or change holes to snap back.

Ball flight, three ways

The pink tracer shows on the hole map, in the tap‑for‑it side profile, and animated over 3D terrain — with yardage stats at the apex.

Carry arcs

Dashed rings show your selected club's reach (and its neighbors) from where you stand. They switch to your learned numbers after a few tracked shots.

Reading the green

GRN shows a deep‑emerald green with white downhill arrows and 1‑ft contours — read the broad break, not 6‑inch putts.

Tournament‑legal

Flip WIND and ± ELEV off for the raw, rules‑legal yardage (USGA Rule 4.3a). Leave both on for everyday golf.

Works offline

Installed to your home screen, the app and your record survive with no signal. Live wind needs a connection.

A little whimsy hides in here for those who explore — long‑press ± ELEV to golf on the Moon, for one.
